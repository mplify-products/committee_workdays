The Four Member Committees

Each committee operates under a formal charter and is led by two designated member volunteers (Committee Co-Chairs).

Commercial and Business Committee (CBC)
Enables and develops pre-standard and standards business and product deliverables supporting Mplify's NaaS standardization and adoption strategy and roadmap.
Carrier Ethernet Wavelength IP Services SD-WAN Cloud Connect IaaS Cybersecurity
Digital Services Committee (DSC)
Defines the service attributes, service definitions, and associated performance monitoring, fault management and implementation guides for services underpinning NaaS offerings.
Carrier Ethernet services Wavelength services IP services Cross connects IaaS
LSO Committee (LSOC)
Defines the key elements of NaaS automation as part of the Lifecycle Service Orchestration framework including product and service schemas, APIs and developer guides.
API requirements/use cases API schemas Product schemas Service schemas MCP reference designs
Test and Certification Committee (TCC)
Oversees the design and governance of Mplify's certification programs, ensuring services and implementations meet published standards.
Carrier Ethernet for Business certification Carrier Ethernet for AI certification SD-WAN certification Cybersecurity certification LSO Test (OIT) service LSO API certification

Organization of Committees

Each committee follows a consistent governance hierarchy from elected leadership through to working group contributors.

Mplify Board of Directors
Elected by membership annually
Co-CEOs
Appointed by Board of Directors annually
Committee Co-Chairs
Designated by Co-CEOs on behalf of Board of Directors
Project Leaders
Self-appointed or staff-appointed
Co-Chair Appointment and Tenure
  • ›Volunteer positions
  • ›Member representatives submit candidacy in May
  • ›Selected candidates appointed in June for two years
  • ›One co-chair appointed per committee each year
Project Leaders
  • ›Member representatives and Mplify contractors
  • ›Start and end of project leadership set in Mplify Portal
  • ›No minimum or maximum tenure

Co-Chair Role and Responsibilities

Committee leadership roles are volunteer positions filled by member company representatives with relevant technical or commercial expertise. All leadership positions are voluntary and unpaid. Mplify Alliance staff provide administrative and editorial support.

Committee Co-Chair
  • ›Aligns the committee vision and roadmap to Mplify's strategic objectives
  • ›Shares chairing duties in committee workday meetings
  • ›Works in close collaboration with Mplify staff
  • ›Promotes member engagement in projects and outreach
  • ›Encourages voting in monthly project ballots
  • ›Initiates and approves decisions in the Co-Chair meetings
  • ›Ensures continuity across committee activities
  • ›Represents the committee to the Co-CEOs
  • ›Manages cross-committee dependencies
  • ›Reports committee progress to the Mplify Board
  • ›Curates projects initiated by members within the committee

Charter Framework

Every committee charter covers the same core sections, ensuring consistency in how committees are defined and held accountable.

🎯

Purpose & Scope

Defines the committee's primary mandate, the areas it is authorised to standardise, and explicit boundaries with adjacent committees to prevent duplication of scope.

📋

Objectives & Deliverables

Lists the expected outputs — standards documents, API specifications, certification test suites — with indicative timelines aligned to the annual workday calendar.

🗹

Voting & Quorum Rules

Specifies the quorum threshold, voting eligibility (Voting Members only), approval majorities for different decision types, and the process for resolving tied votes.

📅

Meeting Cadence

Establishes minimum meeting frequency — typically monthly online calls plus attendance at Mplify Member Workdays — and requirements for agenda publication and minute distribution.

🤝

Membership & Participation

Defines participation rights by membership tier, attendance expectations for Voting Members, and how contributors from other organisations may engage with working groups.

🔄

Charter Review & Amendment

Charters are reviewed annually by the Chair Council. Amendments require a two-thirds majority vote of Voting Members and ratification by the Mplify Board.

Governance & Operations

Decision making in the committees is managed in monthly ballots with consistent rules across all the committees with an emphasis on transparency, fairness and speed of decision-making.

Voting
  • ›Ballots are held for one week starting on the last Monday of every calendar month
  • ›Only Principal Members, Operator Plus Members and Operator Basic Members are entitled to vote
  • ›One vote per voting company regardless of delegate count
  • ›Required majority to pass ballots depends on ballot type
  • ›All ballots are held online
Meeting Operations
  • ›Agendas for meetings are not required in advance
  • ›Recordings, transcriptions and AI summaries are optional for meetings but available for all members if implemented
Deliverable Process
  • ›Committees manage projects to create deliverables
  • ›These deliverables become Mplify assets if approved in ballots
  • ›Committee co-chairs oversee the projects and the eventual deliverables

Getting Involved

Member companies are encouraged to participate actively — from contributing to working groups through to standing for elected Co-Chair positions.

1

Attend Committee Plenaries

All member company representatives may attend monthly plenary sessions. Register via the Mplify Member Portal. Meeting links are distributed to registered attendees.

2

Join a Working Group

Express interest to a Committee Co-Chair. WG participation is open to all member tiers and is the primary way contributions are made to standards drafts.

3

Stand for Co-Chair

Nominations open 60 days before the AGM. Any Voting Member company representative with 12+ months participation is eligible to stand for a Co-Chair position.
